Nephrology Education

Understanding kidney health and treatment options.

What is Nephrology?

Nephrology is an essential medical specialty that deals with the kidneys, which are crucial organs responsible for filtering waste products, regulating blood pressure, and maintaining fluid balance.

Nephrologists diagnose and manage conditions such as:

  • Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
  • Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)
  • Hypertension
  • Electrolyte Imbalances
  • Kidney Stones
  • Glomerulonephritis

🩸 Haemodialysis

The most common form of dialysis. Blood is filtered through an artificial kidney machine (dialyzer) to remove waste and excess fluids.

How it works: Blood is drawn from a vascular access (like an AV fistula), cleaned in the machine using diffusion and ultrafiltration, and returned to the body.

In-Center Blood Filtration AV Fistula

🏠 Peritoneal Dialysis

A home-based treatment option. Uses the body's own peritoneal membrane in the abdomen as a natural filter.

How it works: Dialysis solution (dialysate) enters the abdomen via a catheter, absorbs wastes, and is then drained. It can be done manually (CAPD) or with a machine at night (APD).
